Conclusion

The answer to the driving question of my research into the potential for online learning technology to promote educational opportunity, which goes hand in hand with the promise of prosperity, is a resounding yes. Resources currently available have the capability to improve the quality of and accessibility to education not just in America, but also in the world. Whether or not educators are ready for it, it will transform the American education system; how quickly this will happen depends in part on how loudly Americans demand change. Educational opportunity remains inextricably linked to economic prosperity and quality of life, and it is doled out unequally in America. Technology has the potential to change this by offering online learning to any person with the desire to learn, regardless of socioeconomic status, proximity to a quality school, or the individual's pace and ability to grasp concepts. While I appreciate the ways in which online resources have augmented my own learning, the top-notch education I received in Deerfield schools would have prepared me for success even if I had never stumbled upon resources such as Khan Academy. My hope is for technology to reach students in America and beyond for whom access to quality education is lacking. All students are equally deserving of the opportunities I enjoy simply by living in Deerfield; bringing online resources to American students to enhance their educational experience is how I hope to see our education system transform.
