Introduction

I became intrigued with the idea and promise of web-based learning when I was a junior in AP computer science. This class, above any other, taught me the value of exploring beyond the classroom as I sought out online tutorials to help work through projects. I was able to find resources to practice new skills and deepen my knowledge. Soon after, I discovered Khan Academy, an online learning website committed to providing a "world class education for anyone anywhere" ("Khan Academy"). I used Salman Khan's online lessons to help get through difficult concepts in calculus and chemistry; I loved how I could replay the lessons if I did not understand them the first time. After seeing Khan featured on "60 Minutes," I read his book, The One World Schoolhouse. Reading his book I recognized that my education, like most students' education, is full of holes. Whether it is because of personal stress, lack of motivation, immaturity or a simple sick day, most students struggle to create the best foundation on which to build their growing knowledge. Salman Khan argues that web-based learning has the potential to correct this problem. Filling in some conceptual gaps for me, a student enrolled in one of the best school districts in the United States, might help me have a more successful high school or college career. More significant, however, is considering the possibility for helping students in underserved school districts lacking resources for identifying and assisting when one has fallen behind. In underserved school districts, falling behind might mean the difference between graduating and going on to college or lifelong struggles against poverty. This discovery has led me to believe in the potential for online, self-paced and self-directed learning to transform education in our country where top-notch Deerfield High School is an anomalous bastion of opportunity rather than the standard for American students.

My goal in this project is to present my research in the form of this website I have developed completely through online learning resources. In doing so, I have educated myself in a self-paced and self-directed way on how to build a website. Doing so combines my passion for computer programming with my interest in the promise of technology in education reform. The website itself displays my research and findings as well as offers links to online learning resources. I also hope to promote the idea that education needs to adapt in this age of information. I believe that my generation has a mandate to embrace the technology available to reform learning and formal education to promote equality of educational opportunity because it goes hand in hand with the opportunity for prosperity.
